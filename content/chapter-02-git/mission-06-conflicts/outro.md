# You survived your first conflict

**Sage** raises both hands in mock celebration.

> "That's it. The thing everyone's terrified of, and you just did it.
> Conflicts aren't a *git problem* - they're a *people problem* git is
> politely making visible. Two humans edited the same line; somebody had
> to pick which version wins, or how they combine. That somebody is the
> person resolving the merge.
>
> You did three things people often forget:
>
> - You **didn't panic** at the rejection - you read the error.
> - You **didn't `git push --force`** - that would have destroyed Captain
>   Q's commit. We'll talk about force-push later, but the rule is: never
>   force-push to a branch other people use.
> - You actually **looked at the file** before committing the resolution.
>
> Tomorrow: cleaning up messy local history with `git rebase -i`."

→ Mission 07 unlocked.

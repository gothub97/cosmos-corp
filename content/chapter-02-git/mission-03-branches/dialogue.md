# Branches: a place to try things

**Sage** spins their chair to face you.

> "Here's the secret to not being scared of git: **branches are cheap, and
> branches are private until you push.** Want to try something risky?
> Make a branch. Hate it? Delete the branch. The main line of work never
> noticed.
>
> A branch is just a movable label that points at a commit. When you make a
> new commit on a branch, the label moves forward. When you switch branches,
> git updates the files in your working tree to match that branch's tip.
>
> We'll do the basic loop:
>
> 1. List the branches you have.
> 2. Make a new one called `feature/intake-form` and jump onto it.
> 3. Commit something on the new branch.
> 4. Switch back to `main` - and notice the file you just added is *gone*
>    from your working tree, because main doesn't know about it yet."
